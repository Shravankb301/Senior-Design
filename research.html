Summary of Semester 1 and 2

Semester 1

Researching various AI tools & narrowing down to specific tools to understand their capacity better (requirement 1):

Our research was initiated by conducting a comprehensive investigation into various AI tools with an autocomplete feature, emphasizing popular tools like GitHub Copilot and Open AI. We consulted various research papers and reports to understand how these AI tools can aid programmers in their daily tasks. Moreover, we scrutinized other AI tools like Kite, Wing, and Tabnine, which are known to offer distinctive features and functionalities to programmers.

GitHub Copilot, developed by GitHub in collaboration with OpenAI, is an AI-powered code generation tool that uses machine learning algorithms to suggest code snippets and complete lines of code based on context and coding patterns [5]. It has garnered significant attention due to its potential to automate coding tasks and enhance developer productivity. Open AI, on the other hand, is a leading AI research organization known for its cutting-edge advancements in natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning. Their GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) models, including GPT-3, have been widely used in various applications, including code generation and auto-completion [9].

The outcomes of our research demonstrate that AI tools offer programmers numerous benefits, such as heightened productivity, reduced coding errors, and timesaving. The autocomplete feature of AI tools provides suggestions to programmers that help improve their coding efficiency and save time. Furthermore, AI tools like GitHub Copilot are capable of automating programming tasks to a considerable degree by generating complete code snippets based on natural language inputs [8].

However, it should be noted that these AI tools have certain limitations. For instance, they may not be capable of comprehending the context of the code, which could result in errors or inappropriate code suggestions. Moreover, these tools may be unable to handle complex coding tasks requiring advanced problem-solving skills and creativity. Additionally, specific AI tools may require significant computational resources, which could be cost-prohibitive for some organizations.

Subsequently, to conduct a focused and comprehensive investigation, we have decided to narrow our research focus to two prominent AI tools in the programming community, namely GitHub Copilot and Open AI. These tools have gained widespread popularity and are known for their advanced code generation and autocompletion capabilities. We believe that a thorough assessment of the capabilities and implications of these tools would be critical to completing our research project.

Deciding our audience pool and categories them

As part of our research on Artificial Intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) autocomplete coding tools, we conducted a testing phase to gain deeper insights into the impact of these tools on the daily life of programmers. To ensure a representative sample, we specifically targeted computer science students enrolled in PFW for Programming courses and with basic coding knowledge. To capture a diverse range of skills and experience levels, we categorized them into four sections based on their academic year - freshmen, sophomores, juniors, and seniors.

The methodology for categorizing our audience was carefully designed by our team, taking into consideration the findings and insights gained from most of our testing and research conducted at PFW. The development of this methodology was based on rigorous analysis and evaluation of various factors, including research interests, expertise, and areas of specialization. This approach allowed us to gather data from various students at different stages of their academic journey, providing a comprehensive understanding of the effectiveness of AI/ML autocomplete coding tools in different educational contexts.

It is important to note that the categorization approach we have implemented was inspired by the may is not suitable for all future testing and research projects. We acknowledge that the dynamic nature of research and evolving requirements of different projects may require adjustments or modifications to our current methodology.

Semester 2

More research on ChatGPT & Changing qurstions for bigger testing

Towards the latter stages of the first semester of our Senior Capstone project, a momentous event occurred, as the advanced language model ChatGPT-3 was unveiled in November 2022. Subsequently, during the following semester, our research team embarked on an in-depth investigation into the capabilities of ChatGPT-3.

ChatGPT is a language model that has been developed by OpenAI, an artificial intelligence (AI) research organization. It is based on the Generative Pre-trained Transformer 3.5 (GPT-3.5) architecture and is specifically designed to generate human-like text responses based on input prompts. ChatGPT has been extensively trained on a large corpus of text data sourced from the internet and other sources, which allows it to generate contextually relevant responses to a wide range of queries. [6]

We selected ChatGPT-3 as our tool of choice due to its clear superiority over other AI tools. ChatGPT, developed by OpenAI, surpasses other artificial intelligence systems with its exceptional capabilities and numerous advantages. Its remarkable natural language processing abilities allow it to generate highly accurate and fluent human-like text responses. This proficiency is a result of its extensive training on a diverse range of sources, resulting in a comprehensive knowledge base that enables it to address a wide range of topics and engage in meaningful conversations across different domains. The contextual understanding of ChatGPT-3 empowers it to comprehend nuanced queries and provide contextually relevant and coherent responses, thereby enhancing its usability and user experience. [6]

Additionally, its adaptability shines through its ability to learn from user interactions, continually improving its responses and overall performance over time. The OpenAI team's dedication to ethics and safety measures ensures that ChatGPT-3 adheres to ethical guidelines and minimizes the risk of generating harmful or biased content. Furthermore, ChatGPT's versatile language generation capabilities have successfully been applied in various applications such as content generation, question answering, language translation, and conversational AI, making it an invaluable tool for chatbot applications, virtual assistants, and customer service scenarios. Overall, ChatGPT-3 stands out as a revolutionary AI tool, redefining the field of conversational artificial intelligence.[6]

Following a meticulous analysis of the preliminary findings and a thorough examination of ChatGPT-3's abilities, we opted to revise our testing methodology. Our primary objective was to evaluate the effectiveness of ChatGPT-3 in responding to questions in various formats. We set out to assess ChatGPT-3's autocomplete feature in accurately answering open-ended text-based questions, in contrast to participants' personal knowledge and unrestricted access to the web. Additionally, we evaluated ChatGPT-3's proficiency in processing visual information and generating precise responses based on visual cues using picture-based questions.

Our testing approach was designed to enable us to evaluate ChatGPT-3's performance under various scenarios and explore its potential applications in natural language processing and other related fields. Our revised methodology allowed us to obtain a more comprehensive evaluation of ChatGPT-3's capabilities and assess its potential usefulness in diverse contexts. By leveraging various question formats and considering participants' prior knowledge and experience, we were able to obtain a more nuanced evaluation of ChatGPT-3's performance.

Overall, our investigation into ChatGPT-3's abilities represented a significant contribution to the field of natural language processing. Our testing approach provided a rigorous evaluation of ChatGPT-3's capabilities, enabling us to gain insights into its potential and limitations. We believe that our findings will contribute to the ongoing development of language models and related applications and pave the way for further research in the field.
